Do You Know How Vital Good Rest Is?

  • Sleeping well is essential for our health and emotional well-being.
  • Getting enough sleep and having good quality sleep are essential for healthy sleep.
  • The amount of sleep you need changes as you age.
  • Talk to your healthcare provider if you have trouble sleeping.

Health benefits of sleep

Get sick less often

Quality sleep strengthens your immune system, helping you fight off viruses and infections.

Maintain a healthy weight

Sleep regulates the hormones that control hunger and appetite, helping prevent weight gain.

Reduce stress and improve your mood

Proper rest helps manage emotions, reduces irritability, and improves emotional stability.

Others Benefits

Improve your heart health and metabolism

Deep sleep supports healthy blood pressure and blood sugar regulation.

Reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as

Type 2 diabetes, Heart disease, Hypertension, Stroke

Reduce the risk of car accidents and injury-related deaths

Drowsy driving slows reaction time and reduces focus, increasing accident risk.

Strategies to improve sleep

Improving sleep habits can help you sleep well. Some habits that can improve your sleep include:

  1. 1. Going to bed and waking up at the same time every day

    Maintaining a consistent sleep schedule helps regulate your internal clock and improves sleep quality.

  2. 2. Keeping your bedroom quiet, relaxing, and cool

    A calm and comfortable environment promotes deeper, uninterrupted sleep.

  3. 3. Turning off electronic devices at least 30 minutes before bedtime

    Blue light from screens can interfere with melatonin production, making it harder to fall asleep.

  4. 4. Avoiding heavy meals and alcohol before bed

    These can disrupt digestion and lead to restless or fragmented sleep.

  5. 5. Avoiding caffeine in the afternoon or evening

    Caffeine is a stimulant that can keep your mind alert when it should be winding down.

  6. 6. Exercising regularly and maintaining a healthy diet

    Physical activity and balanced nutrition contribute to more restorative and high-quality sleep.

Signs of poor sleep quality include:

  • Difficulty falling asleep: Taking more than 20–30 minutes to fall asleep may be due to stress, anxiety, screen use before bed, or an irregular bedtime routine.
  • Waking up repeatedly during the night: Frequent awakenings disrupt the natural sleep cycle and may be linked to anxiety, sleep apnea, or an uncomfortable environment.
  • Feeling sleepy or tired even after enough sleep: Even after 7 to 9 hours, fatigue may indicate poor sleep quality, undiagnosed disorders, or issues like depression or hormonal imbalances.

Sleep Disorders

Some people may have health problems that prevent them from sleeping well, no matter how hard they try. These problems are called sleep disorders. Some of this common sleep disorders are:

Insomnia

A sleep disorder characterized by difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking up too early, leading to daytime fatigue and concentration problems.

Restless Leg Syndrome

A condition causing an uncontrollable urge to move the legs, usually in the evening or night, often disrupting sleep and causing discomfort.

Narcolepsy

A chronic neurological disorder that affects the brain's ability to regulate sleep–wake cycles, causing sudden sleep attacks and excessive daytime sleepiness.

Sleep Apnea

A serious condition where breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep, often leading to poor sleep, poor sleep quality, and increased health risks.

Keeping a sleep diary

Your sleep diary should include the times when you:

Time you go to bed

Wake up during the night

Take medications

Exercise

Take naps

Consume alcohol or caffeinated drinks
