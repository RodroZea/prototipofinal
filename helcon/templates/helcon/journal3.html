How Can Stress Be Managed?

Learn to Manage Stress

The World Health Organization (WHO) created "Doing What Matters in Times of Stress: An Illustrated Guide" to help people manage stress through simple, evidence-based techniques. This self-help guide is designed for anyone experiencing stress, core strategies that can be practiced in just a few minutes a day. These include grounding yourself in the present moment and making space for difficult emotions.

Follow a Daily Routine

Doing a series of activities each day can help you feel more in control of your life and be more effective. Schedule specific times to eat, spend time with family, do daily tasks, and engage in physical or leisure activities.

Simple Tips for a Healthy, Balanced Life

Adopting habits like getting enough sleep, eating well, exercising, and staying socially connected can improve your physical and emotional well-being.
Take care of your body and mind with these simple daily tips.

Get Enough Sleep

Sleep is vital for both the body and mind. Good sleep repairs, relaxes, and revitalizes the body and helps cope with stress.

Good sleep hygiene includes

  • Consistency: Going to bed and waking up at the same time every day, even on weekends.
  • Sleep in a quiet, dark and comfortable environment at a comfortable temperature.
  • Avoid electronic devices before bedtime.
  • Avoid caffeine, heavy meals, or coffee before bed.
  • Exercise during the day can help you fall asleep more easily at night.

Stay Connected with Others

Talk with family and friends about your concerns and feelings.
Staying connected can help you feel more positive and less alone.

Eat a Healthy Diet

Everything you eat and drink affects your health.
Eat balanced meals at regular intervals, stay hydrated, and try to
include plenty of fresh fruits and vegetables.

Exercise Regularly

Daily exercise can help reduce stress, be fun in leisure time, or simply walking.

Limit News Consumption

Watching or reading too much news on TV or social media can cause stress.If you notice that news consumption causes stress, limit the time you spend on it.
