Preventive Care

Primary Health Care (PHC) is a comprehensive health approach that aims to guarantee the well-being of individuals throughout their entire life, from health promotion and disease prevention to treatment, rehabilitation, and palliative care.Its purpose is to ensure an equitable distribution of health, providing care centered on the needs of individuals as close as possible to their everyday environment.

Health Equity

PHC promotes social justice, equity, and solidarity, considering that access to the highest attainable level of health is a fundamental right for all. To achieve universal health coverage (UHC), health systems must focus on individuals and work with their participation, extending health into all policies and addressing the social determinants of health.

PHC has three interdependent and synergistic components

  1. Integrated health services: Covering primary and public health functions.
  2. policies: Aimed at addressing the broader determinants of health.
  3. Community empowerment: people, families, and communities to foster social participation and improve self-sufficiency in health.

Primary Health Care (PHC) is
crucial for several reasons

  • Inclusivity and equity: PHC is the most inclusive and equitable approach to improving physical, mental, and social health, ensuring access to all sectors of the population, especially the most vulnerable.
  • Improvement of health equity: Investments in PHC help improve access to health services, reducing inequalities in healthcare and improving health outcomes.
  • Cost-effectiveness and efficiency: Investing in PHC is cost-effective and efficiently improves health system performance, optimizing resources and reducing long-term costs.
  • Resilience in times of crisis: PHC enables health systems to be more resilient during crises, such as the COVID-19 pandemic. It helps detect early signs of epidemics and allows for a quicker response to increases in service demand.
  • Social and environmental factors: PHC acknowledges that health depends not only on medical services but also on factors like education, social protection, food systems, and the environment — all of which are key to overall well-being.
