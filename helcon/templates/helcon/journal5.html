Risk Factors Addressed

  • Tobacco use: Smoking significantly increases the risk of heart disease and strokes.
  • Unhealthy diet: Diets high in saturated fats, sugars, and salt, and low in fruits and vegetables.
  • Harmful alcohol consumption: Excessive drinking can raise blood pressure and contribute to heart disease.
  • Physical inactivity: Lack of regular exercise can lead to overweight and other health issues.

These factors are interrelated with physiological conditions like hypertension, elevated cholesterol and glucose levels, and obesity.

Intervention Tools and Strategies

  • Find out: Inquire about the patient’s habits.
  • Advise: Provide clear and personalized information.
  • Appreciate: Assess the patient’s willingness to change.
  • Assist: Offer support and strategies for change.
  • Arrange: Schedule follow-ups and reinforce commitment.

Additionally, the motivational interviewing technique is emphasized as a way to encourage behavior change, adapting interventions to the patient’s cultural and social context.

Those who work on this are

  • Primary healthcare professionals: Doctors, nurses, and other healthcare workers who interact directly with patients.
  • Trainers: Those responsible for training healthcare staff on prevention and control of cardiovascular diseases (CVD).
  • Health program managers: Those responsible for planning and overseeing disease prevention initiatives.
