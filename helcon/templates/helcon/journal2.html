Benefits of Physical Activity

Regular physical activity is very beneficial for both physical and mental health.

  • In adults: helps prevent and control non-communicable diseases like cardiovascular diseases, cancer, and diabetes; reduces symptoms of depression and anxiety; and promotes brain health and overall well-being.
  • In children and adolescents: promotes bone health, stimulates healthy growth and development of muscles, and improves motor and cognitive development.

Specific benefits include

  • In children and adolescents: Improves fitness, cardiometabolic and bone health, cognitive capacity, and mental health, and reduces body fat.
  • In adults and the elderly: Reduces the risk of all-cause mortality and cardiovascular disease mortality, the occurrence of hypertension, specific types of cancer, and type 2 diabetes; prevents falls; and improves mental health, cognitive health, sleep, and body fat measures.
  • In women during pregnancy and postpartum: Reduces the risk of preeclampsia, gestational hypertension, gestational diabetes, excessive weight gain during pregnancy, childbirth complications, postpartum depression, and postnatal complications. Additionally, physical activity has no adverse effects on birth weight nor increases the risk of prenatal death.
